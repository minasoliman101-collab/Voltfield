Part ID · Photo Identification

Photograph the nameplate. We'll find the part.

Upload photos of a failed part's nameplate, rating label, or markings. The label is read on your device (nothing is uploaded anywhere), the manufacturer, ratings and model numbers are extracted, and you get ranked replacement options — with obsolete-platform alerts, one-click component BOM teardowns for parent assemblies, and a straight path into the PCB Builder when it's a board.

1 · Add photos

Nameplates, rating labels, stampings, PCB silkscreen — the sharper and flatter the label, the better the read. On a phone, "Take photo" opens the camera.

2 · Read the label

On-device OCR (loads a ~3 MB engine on first use). You can also skip the photo and type whatever you can read off the part.

3 · Matches

Add a photo or type the label text, then hit Identify part. Results show replacement options, EOL alerts, BOM teardowns, and PCB routing.
How this works & what's next: identification runs entirely in your browser — OCR reads the label, and the text is matched against 236 part families, 21 obsolete-platform records, and 22 OEM lines. Photos never leave your device. Roadmap: when a vision backend is configured (README §11), bare-part photos — no label needed — will be recognized directly, parent assemblies will auto-expand into their component BOMs, and photographed boards will pre-fill the PCB Builder. The hooks are already in place.
